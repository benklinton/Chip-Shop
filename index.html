FAQ

What is a rock chip? A “rock chip”, is usually created after an object strikes your vehicle’s laminated glass and creates a damage spot. In most vehicles only your windshield is laminated, but some side windows can be laminated as well. If the object strikes hard enough the glass will separate under the surface and create an air pocket in the glass, also known as a crack. If the damage is left untreated the glass will continue to separate resulting in the glass having to be removed and replaced. The majority of the time a rock is the object flying through the air, thus the name “rock chip”.

How do I know if my windshield needs to be repaired? Our windshield take a beating daily, especially here in Utah. Luckley that is what they are designed to do. After the glass is hit a damage spot is created from the glass chipping away. Most of the time the damage spot is just a surface chip or a “pit”. The pitting of the windshield is nothing to be alarmed of on their own. Though it may be an eyesore the integrity of the glass has not been compromised. If the pit has a black or greyish coloring; around, under, or even sometimes next to it; that means that the subsurface of the glass has separated and needs to be repaired. When separation occurs, an air pocket is created which will cause a shadow to be cast in the chip. That is where your black or greyish coloring comes from. It is definitely easier to spot the separation in larger chips like star or bullseye breaks, but some cracks can be very small measuring only 1⁄4” and hard to see, even these will eventually spread. Once repaired, the separated portion of the glass is filled in causing the shadow to disappear making the damage less vizable and the glass as solid as glass can get.
